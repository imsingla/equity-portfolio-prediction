Comparable sales for the first quarter of 2015 increased 5.2%, with comparable sales of 5.1% in February, 6.6% in March, and 3.8% in April.  Net earnings for the first quarter increased 7.8% to $673 million, and diluted earnings per share increased 14.8% to $0.70 per share. While the disruption at the west coast ports added complexity to the spring preparation, our transportation and logistics teams successfully collaborated to re-route product to other ports, minimizing the impact these challenges created. In addition, lower energy prices should fuel stronger consumer spending in 2015.  We also continue to see steady recovery within the housing market, including positive trends in turnover and moderate home price appreciation as measured by the Federal Housing Finance Agency.
