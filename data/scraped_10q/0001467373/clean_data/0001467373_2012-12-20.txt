Our Health & Public Service operating group experienced strong consulting revenue growth in local currency during the first quarter of fiscal 2013. Year-over-year consulting revenue growth in local currency moderated or declined for our other four operating groups, as we experienced a slight year-over-year decline in both systems integration and management consulting services, driven principally by declines in EMEA. In our consulting business in these four operating groups, clients reduced their demand for small and medium size projects compared to the first quarter of fiscal 2012. In addition, while we experienced year-over-year increased demand for larger transformational projects, they were of longer duration and are converting to revenue at a slower rate. These trends have led to lower consulting revenue growth and/or declines in most areas of our business, and we expect this to continue in the near term. Clients continued to be focused on initiatives designed to deliver cost savings and operational efficiency, as well as projects to integrate their global operations and grow and transform their businesses. We are also experiencing growing demand for our services in emerging technologies, including analytics, cloud computing and mobility. If an entity determines that this is the case, it is required to perform the currently prescribed two-step goodwill impairment test to identify potential goodwill impairment and measure the amount of goodwill impairment loss to be recognized for that reporting unit (if any). If an entity determines that the fair value of a reporting unit is greater than its carrying amount, the two-step goodwill impairment test is not required.
